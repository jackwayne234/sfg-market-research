# Regulatory Mandates Creating Demand

| Regulation | Scope | Relevance to SFG |
|---|---|---|
| EPA 40 CFR 63 | Refinery fence-line monitoring (benzene) | Open-path SFG for perimeter detection |
| EPA methane rule (2024) | Oil and gas methane from new + existing sources | Continuous monitoring mandate |
| OSHA 29 CFR 1910.146 | Confined space entry: O2, combustibles, H2S, CO | Multi-gas detection requirement |
| MSHA 30 CFR Part 75 | Underground mine methane/CO monitoring | High-reliability continuous detection |
| IEEE 344-2013 | Seismic qualification for nuclear equipment | Solid-state passes more easily than FTIR |
| NRC post-Fukushima FLEX | Beyond-design-basis event mitigation | Monitoring must survive the event |
| NFPA 470/472 | HazMat responder equipment standards | First responder detection capability |

## Key Regulatory Drivers

### EPA Methane Rule (2024)
Mandates methane monitoring for oil and gas facilities, including existing sources. Creates massive demand for continuous monitoring devices. Alternative detection technologies (drones, continuous monitors) explicitly approved.

### Post-Fukushima Seismic Requirements
After Fukushima, regulations got stricter about what monitoring equipment must survive in seismic zones. If your gas monitor keeps working through a 6.0 magnitude earthquake while the FTIR goes out of alignment and stops reporting, that's not a convenience — that's the difference between detecting a gas leak caused by the earthquake and not detecting it.

### MSHA Mining Safety
500 consecutive successful tests required for MSHA certification. Equipment must withstand vibration from blasting, dust, and water intrusion. Catalytic bead sensors get poisoned by H2S and silicone — common in mines.

### EPA Fence-Line Monitoring
Refineries must monitor hazardous air pollutants at the facility perimeter. Currently served by open-path FTIR systems that require regular maintenance of the scanning mirror.

## Regulatory Pitch

Mandate solid-state monitors for facilities within radius of:
- Fault lines (seismic zones)
- Volcanic zones
- Tsunami-prone coastlines
- Hurricane paths

**Argument:** The monitoring system must survive the event it's monitoring for. Moving-mirror instruments fail when shaken. Solid-state doesn't.

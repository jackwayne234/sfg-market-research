# Durability Value Proposition

## Core Message

**The event that breaks your monitor is the same event that causes the leak.**

An earthquake shakes a refinery. Gas lines crack. The FTIR monitor — with its precision-aligned scanning mirror — loses alignment and stops reporting. The gas leak goes undetected at the exact moment detection matters most.

The SFG device has no mirror. No moving parts. Nothing to shake loose. It keeps reporting through the earthquake, through the aftershocks, through the recovery.

## Why Solid-State Matters

Every FTIR spectrometer contains a Michelson interferometer with a mirror that must move with sub-micron precision. This is the core of the instrument — without it, nothing works. In a controlled lab, this is fine. On an offshore platform with constant engine vibration, or in a mine with blasting, or in a seismic zone, this is a liability.

The SFG device replaces the interferometer with a fixed PPLN crystal array. Phase matching is a property of the crystal lattice — it doesn't change with vibration, temperature cycling, or impacts. The selectivity is built into the atomic structure.

## Environment-Specific Arguments

### Offshore Platforms
"Your FTIR needs a climate-controlled enclosure and a helicopter technician every time the mirror drifts. Our device sits in a sealed housing on the deck and keeps working. How many helicopter visits per year do you schedule for FTIR maintenance?"

### Mining
"Your catalytic bead sensors get poisoned by H2S and silicone within months. Our PPLN crystal is lithium niobate — the same material in your cell phone RF filters. It doesn't react with anything in your mine atmosphere."

### Seismic Zones
"IEEE 344 seismic qualification requires equipment to function during and after design-basis earthquakes. A device with no moving parts passes that test more easily than one with a precision-aligned scanning mirror."

### Military/Field
"Your troops carry detection equipment through mud, rain, and combat. They drop it. They throw it in the back of a truck. The JCAD works because it's ruggedized. But it can't detect toxic industrial chemicals. Our device is equally rugged and detects what JCAD misses."

### First Responders
"Your HazMat team's handheld FTIR costs $7,590 and breaks when a firefighter drops it in turnout gear. Ours doesn't have a mirror to break."

## Regulatory Angle

Post-Fukushima regulations require monitoring equipment to survive beyond-design-basis events. The logical extension: all gas monitoring equipment in seismic zones should be rated to survive the events it's designed to detect.

Pitch to EPA/NRC: Mandate solid-state gas monitors for facilities within a defined radius of fault lines, volcanic zones, tsunami-prone coastlines, and hurricane paths.
